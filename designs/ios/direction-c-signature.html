Give me a quick overview of what NTRP is and its main subsystems.

NTRP is your personal entropy-reduction agent — a single-user assistant that runs tools, keeps memory, and executes scheduled tasks. The core pieces:

  • Agent loop — spawns runs, executes tools, streams events over SSE.
  • Memory — fact-centric: facts to entities to observations, plus cross-domain dreams.
  • Tools — bash, files, web, email, calendar, and hybrid search.

Can you dial my 20V supply down to about 5V for the driver stage?

A few clean ways to drop 20V to 5V, depending on what you have on hand:

  • Buck converter — the right tool here. Cheap, efficient, no heat.
  • Resistor in series — wastes power as heat, fine for testing only.
  • PWM the input — your 555 already does this; lower the duty cycle.
